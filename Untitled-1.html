Cyber Safety Tips

  • Use Strong Passwords: Create complex passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and special characters.
  • Change Passwords Regularly: Update your passwords periodically.
  • Avoid Reusing Passwords: Use different passwords for different accounts.
  • Keep Software Updated: Regularly update your operating system, browser, and software to protect against vulnerabilities.
  • Install Antivirus and Antimalware: Use reliable security software and keep it up-to-date.
  • Be Wary of Phishing Scams: Don’t click on suspicious links or attachments in emails.
  • Verify Websites: Ensure websites are secure (look for “https” and a padlock icon in the address bar) before entering personal information.
  • Limit Sharing Personal Information: Avoid sharing sensitive information on social media and other online platforms.
  • Use Secure Connections: Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions; use a VPN if necessary.
  • Secure Your Home Network: Change the default password on your router and enable encryption.

Types of Cyber Scams

  • Phishing scams: fraudulent emails, texts, or messages that trick you into revealing sensitive information
  • Ransomware attacks: malware that encrypts your files and demands payment in exchange for the decryption key
  • Identity theft: stealing personal information to impersonate you or commit fraud
  • Online auction fraud: scams that target online shoppers, often through fake websites or sellers
  • Investment scams: fraudulent schemes that promise high returns on investments
